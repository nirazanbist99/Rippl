Global Water Accessibility

Addressing the inequitable distribution of clean water resources and exploring solutions for universal access by 2030.

The Access Gap

Despite significant progress, 785 million people still lack basic drinking water services. The disparity is stark: while urban populations in developed nations enjoy near-universal access to treated water, rural communities in sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia often rely on untreated surface water sources. Climate change is exacerbating these inequalities, with shifting rainfall patterns and glacial retreat threatening established water supply systems.

Barriers to Access

Multiple interconnected factors prevent equitable water access:

  • Infrastructure Deficit: Many developing regions lack the piping, treatment plants, and storage facilities needed for reliable supply.
  • Economic Barriers: The poorest households often pay 10-20 times more per litre than wealthy households connected to mains supply.
  • Governance Challenges: Corruption, lack of political will, and inefficient utilities hinder progress toward universal coverage.
  • Climate Vulnerability: Regions dependent on single water sources face catastrophic risk from droughts and floods.
  • Gender Inequality: Women and girls in rural areas spend an estimated 200 million hours daily collecting water globally.

Innovative Solutions

Emerging technologies and governance models are proving effective in closing the access gap. Solar-powered desalination units bring affordable freshwater to coastal communities. Blockchain-based water credit systems enable transparent tracking of water rights and usage in shared basins. Decentralised treatment systems—such as biosand filters and solar disinfection (SODIS)—empower households to treat their own water at negligible cost.

Looking Forward

Achieving universal water access by 2030 requires tripling current rates of investment. The estimated cost is $114 billion per year, but the economic returns—through reduced disease burden and increased productivity—would far exceed this investment.
